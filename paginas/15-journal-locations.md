## COPY (artículo)

**H1:** The Best Places to Take Pictures in the Tri-Cities

After twenty years of photographing families, seniors and couples across Richland, Kennewick and Pasco, I've learned that the "best" location isn't the most famous one — it's the one where the light, the season and your story line up. Here's my honest local guide, including when each spot glows and who it fits best.

**H2: How I Choose a Location (It's Always About the Light)**

Every location on this list earns its place for one reason: how it holds golden hour. Eastern Washington gives us dry air, low hills and long evenings — which means the last hour before sunset here is some of the most beautiful portrait light anywhere. When we plan your session, I don't just pick a pretty place; I pick where the light will be doing something special at the exact time we shoot.

**H2: Richland Photo Locations**

**H3: 1. Howard Amon Park**
Riverfront lawns, mature trees and soft light coming off the Columbia. Ideal for families with little ones — room to run, shade to reset in, and water light that flatters everyone. `[LISA: detalle de primera mano]`

**H3: 2. The Columbia River paths**
The walking paths along the river give us movement — strolling shots, hand-holding, kids on shoulders — with willow and water backdrops that change beautifully by season.

**H3: 3. Badger Mountain foothills**
Open sky, dry golden grasses and that wide-horizon feel. My pick for seniors and couples who want warm, editorial light without a building in sight.

**H3: 4. Chamna Natural Preserve**
Wilder and quieter — trails, brush and river access that feel a world away from town. For families who'd rather explore than pose.

**H2: Kennewick Photo Locations**

**H3: 5. Columbia Park**
The Tri-Cities' classic for a reason: big lawns, big trees, shoreline and endless variety. If your crew spans grandparents to toddlers, this is the easy, beautiful answer.

**H3: 6. The riverfront at sunset**
West-facing water views mean the sun sets *into* your photos — silhouettes, glow and sparkle on the Columbia.

**H3: 7. The golden hills at the city's edge**
Dry grass that turns to honey at golden hour. Senior sessions here feel like album covers.

**H3: 8. Downtown Kennewick**
Murals, brick and storefronts for seniors and branding sessions that want texture and personality instead of nature.

**H2: Pasco Photo Locations**

**H3: 9. Sacajawea State Park**
Where the Snake meets the Columbia — water on two sides, driftwood and open sky. Worth the short drive every single time.

**H3: 10. Orchards and farmland**
Rows of trees, blossoms in spring, dusty gold in late summer. `[LISA: confirmar acceso/permisos de los huertos que usa]`

**H3: 11. Open country roads**
The long-road-at-sunset shot every senior asks about? This is where we take it.

**H2: 12. The Best Location of All: A Place That's Yours**

Some of my favorite sessions have happened in backyards, on grandparents' land, and in places you'd drive past without a second look. If a spot already holds a memory for your family, it will always beat a "pretty" location. Bring me somewhere that matters to you — I'll find the light when we get there.

**H2: Which Location Fits Your Session?**

- **Families with young kids:** Howard Amon or Columbia Park — room to run, easy parking, shade.
- **Seniors:** Badger foothills, downtown Kennewick, country roads — depending on your style.
- **Couples:** riverfront at sunset, Sacajawea, the hills.
- **Newborns:** usually your own home — the most meaningful location there is. → `/journal/in-home-vs-studio-newborn-photography/`
- **Branding:** your workspace first, then downtown or a location that matches your brand. → `/branding-photographer-tri-cities-wa/`
