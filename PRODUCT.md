# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

The primary audience is people across Richland, Kennewick, and Pasco who want photographs of a meaningful season without having to perform for the camera: high school seniors and their parents, families, new parents, and local business owners. They arrive unsure how to pose, what to wear, or where to go, and need to feel personally guided before they are ready to inquire.

## Product Purpose

The site introduces Lisa Weiss and the experience of working with It’s A Keeper Photography, answers the practical questions that create hesitation, and guides the right visitor into a personal session inquiry. Success means the visitor feels seen, understands what Lisa photographs and how the experience works, and reaches out with confidence.

## Positioning

Warm, unhurried photography that preserves people as they really feel, guided by someone who has spent twenty years following the Tri-Cities light.

## Conversion & proof

- The primary CTA is to plan a session through the inquiry experience; the secondary path is to learn through the relevant service, investment, location, or journal page.
- The line a visitor should remember after ten seconds is “keep the way this felt.”
- The belief ladder is: Lisa understands why these photographs matter; she will make the session feel natural; she knows the people, light, and locations of the Tri-Cities; the experience is carefully guided; reaching out will feel personal and low-pressure.
- Proof currently on hand includes twenty years behind the camera, fourteen years in business, the deployed portfolio and approved photographs, and three existing testimonial themes. Review count, attribution, awards, credentials, and other bracketed claims remain pending verification and must not be invented.

## Brand Personality

Warm, unhurried, and archival. The voice is personal and gently confident, with enough practical clarity to make nervous clients feel held. The emotional goal is the calm of golden hour and the intimacy of handling a print that already feels important.

## Anti-references

This must not resemble a pale light-and-airy photography template, a generic wedding theme, a repeated grid of equal marketing cards, or an assembled SEO site whose pages all share the same section sequence. It must also avoid bright or cool accents, pure black and white UI, rounded cards, gradients, glass effects, decorative shadows, busy motion, and invented proof.

## Design Principles

1. Let photographs carry the emotional weight and let the interface behave like the paper, line, and pacing around a physical print.
2. Preserve the approved Netlify homepage as the strongest design and behavioral authority; vary new pages against it, never away from it.
3. Guide before asking: resolve practical uncertainty in Lisa’s exact words before presenting the inquiry path.
4. Use asymmetry, crossing construction lines, arches, and photographic overlaps as structural devices, not decoration.
5. Publish only what is true and ready; unresolved facts and media stay visibly registered in code and gated from search.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

All content and conversion paths must remain usable by keyboard, retain visible focus, use comfortably legible contrast on every surface and state, preserve at least 16px body copy, provide meaningful alternative text, and respect reduced-motion preferences. Dark surfaces must remain readable under low screen brightness, and responsive compositions must not sacrifice content order or interaction clarity.
