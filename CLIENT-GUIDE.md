# Your Website, In Your Hands

Hi Lisa! You can update the words, photos, testimonials, and business details on
your website without changing its design.

## Sign in

1. Visit `https://www.itsakeeperphotography.com/admin`.
2. Sign in with your own TinaCloud account.
3. Open **Homepage** to edit the page visually.

Only people invited to the TinaCloud project can edit or save the site. Your role is
**Editor**, so you can manage content without access to technical project settings.

## Edit directly on the page

Move your pointer over the preview and click the words or photo you want to change.
The matching field opens in the left sidebar. Text changes appear in the preview
while you type, before you save.

You can also use the left menu:

- **Homepage** contains every section from top to bottom.
- **Kind Words (Testimonials)** contains client quotes.
- **Business Info & Settings** contains your phone, address, links, logo, and SEO details.

## Change words

1. Click the text in the page preview or select its section in the sidebar.
2. Type the new wording.
3. Click **Save**.

The editor checks important length limits so long copy cannot accidentally break the
layout.

## Change a photo

1. Click the photo or its image field.
2. Choose an existing photo or upload a new one.
3. Complete the photo description field.
4. Click **Save**.

Use vertical photos for portrait cards and wide photos for banners. New uploads are
automatically resized for phones and computers during the next site deployment.

## Add a testimonial

1. Open **Kind Words (Testimonials)**.
2. Click **Create New**.
3. Add the quote, first name, and session type.
4. Turn on **Show on the homepage** when it should be featured.
5. Click **Save**.

The homepage displays the first three featured testimonials.

## Hide a section

Each homepage section includes **Show this section on the site**. Turn it off to hide
the section without deleting it. Turn it on later to restore it.

## What happens after Save

TinaCloud stores the change in the website's GitHub history and starts a new site
deployment. The editor preview updates immediately; the public website usually
updates within a few minutes.

Every saved version is kept in history, so an earlier version can be restored if
needed.
